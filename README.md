# ScholarPath

A fictional case study created as part of my Business Analysis portfolio. ScholarPath is a concept for a Nigerian EdTech scholarship platform, designed to connect students with available scholarship opportunities while giving institutions a structured way to manage applications. This project simulates the documentation a Business Analyst would produce when defining and scoping a new platform from the ground up.

## Solution

The response to this project was a formal **Business Requirements Document (BRD)** — the foundational artifact that translates a business problem into clearly defined scope, objectives, and functional requirements before any design or development work begins. Alongside the ScholarPath-specific BRD, a reusable **BRD template** was also developed to standardize how future business requirements are captured and structured.

## Document architecture

**01 — ScholarPath BRD**
- Business problem and project objectives defining why a scholarship platform is needed and what success looks like
- Scope definition outlining what is included and explicitly excluded from the project
- Functional and non-functional requirements covering core platform capabilities (e.g. student registration, scholarship listings, application tracking)
- Stakeholder and assumption analysis to surface dependencies and risks early

**02 — Business Requirement Document (BRD) Template**
- A reusable BRD structure built from industry-standard sections (objectives, scope, requirements, assumptions, sign-off)
- Designed to standardize how requirements are documented across future projects
- Demonstrates the ability to not only complete BA deliverables, but also build repeatable frameworks for consistency
